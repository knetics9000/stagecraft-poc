## The Framework Integration — How to Talk About It Tomorrow

Faisal will probably want to lead with the framework idea because it's fresh and exciting to him. Let him. Then steer the conversation so the framework integration becomes one *supporting* element of the strategy, not the centerpiece.

**Say this when the framework topic comes up:**

> "Your instinct on MEDDIC, SPIN, and BANT is right and I updated the spec for it — Section 3.5. It makes the POC demo land immediately because customers don't have to define their own criteria. The thing I want to flag is that Coffee AI already does this — auto-applying MEDDIC and BANT to meeting data is their headline feature. So the frameworks bring us to parity with them, they don't put us ahead. Our actual differentiation has to stay rooted in three things: rep narrative input instead of meeting recordings, vertical focus instead of horizontal, and management visibility as the hero. The frameworks support those — they don't replace them."

If Faisal pushes back ("but we'll do MEDDIC better"):

> "Maybe. But 'we do MEDDIC better than Coffee AI' is not a sentence a sales leader buys on. 'We're the only AI sales OS for [biotech BD / EdTech enrollment / your-vertical]' is a sentence they buy on. Let's not let a good idea like the frameworks substitute for the harder strategic conversation about who we're for."

## Tough Moments — Prepared Responses

**If Faisal says "let's just start building, we'll figure out the rest as we go":**

> "I can't do that. Two independent simulations both said the projects that die in 6 months are exactly the ones where partners started building before locking down the partnership terms and the strategic decisions. I'm asking you to do the hard 90 minutes now so we don't waste the next 9 months."

**If Faisal won't pick a single vertical:**

> "Horizontal is what kills projects like this. Both well-funded competitors are horizontal — we can't beat them at their own game. We pick one. We can be wrong about which one, but we can't be vague about it."

**If Faisal won't go full-time within 2-3 months:**

> "That's not a deal-breaker, but it changes what we're building. A part-time founder builds a different project than a full-time founder. Tell me which version of this we're doing — slower, smaller, or full ambition with full commitment — so I know what I'm signing up to."

**If Faisal won't commit to written partnership terms:**

> "Every co-founder dispute I've seen starts with 'we didn't think we needed to write it down.' I'd rather have an awkward 30 minutes now than a project-ending argument in month 6. This is for both of us, including the friendship."

**If you genuinely can't reach agreement:**

> "I'd rather we figure out today that this isn't going to work the way we'd hoped than spend 6 months figuring it out the hard way. The idea is real, the friendship is real, and there's no shame in deciding the project isn't right today. Let's call it cleanly and stay friends."
